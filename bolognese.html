THE ODIN RECIPES

BOLOGNESE

Ingredients

  • - Cabernet Sauvignon (1/2 bottle)
  • - 21/2 lbs ground beef and pork, 80/20
  • - 1 Large onion, diced
  • - 2 Carrots diced
  • - 3 Celery stalks diced
  • - 5 Cloves garlic
  • - 2 Cups tomato paste
  • - 3-6 Bay leaves
  • - 1 Bunch of thyme
  • - Olive Oil
  • - Parmegiano Reggiano
  • - 1/4 Cup cream
  • - Pasta, of your choice
  • - Salt and pepper, to taste
  • - Water, as needed
  • - Fresh parsley, for garnish

Steps:

  1. Cut veggies into a rough dice
  2. Blend, but not puree, each vegetable in a food processor - blend garlic and onions together and add them into a bowl. Carrots and celery should be pulsed into a fine, coarse texture mimicking ground beef.
  3. Use a large, heavy-bottom pan preheated at medium-high heat. Coat bottom with oil and add vegetables. Season generously with salt and pepper, cook for 20 minutes to sweat out all the moisture until browning (a.k.a. 'Fond') occurs at bottom of the pan.
  4. Add meat. Break meat up and season generously, combined with vegetables. Cook until moisture is dissipated and more browning occurs.
  5. Add 2 cups of tomato paste and mix well. Lower the heat and cook for another 3-5 minutes until fond on the bottom of the pan is deepened (but not burnt).
  6. One fond is developed, deglaze with at least half a bottle of red wine. Use a wooden spoon to scrape the bottom of the pan clean. Cook until wine is reduced by half or more. Taste for seasoning.
  7. Add bay leaves and thyme. Add enough water to cover the meat. Simmer and cook for 3 to 4 hours.
  8. Check every hour to see if more water is needed, 1 cup at a time, and then cook down.
  9. Add cream and Parmegiano Reggiano once thoroughly cooked; remove herbs.
